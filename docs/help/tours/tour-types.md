# Tour Types

When creating a tour you must select a **Tour Type**. The type is informational — it helps organise your tours and appears on your public site — but does not restrict what you can configure.

## Available Types

### Single Dive

A trip with one dive, typically 45–60 minutes underwater. Common for reef dives, wreck dives, or beginner experiences.

### Multi Dive

A trip with two or more dives. Examples: morning two-tank reef dive, three-tank liveaboard day trip. Use the **Duration** field to reflect the total time on the water.

### Course

A structured learning experience — Open Water, Advanced, Rescue, or specialty certifications. If you use the Training module for formal course management, you may prefer to use the **Training → Courses** section instead. Tours of type Course are still bookable through the regular booking flow.

### Snorkel

Surface snorkelling experience. No certification required. Suitable for families and non-divers.

### Night Dive

A dive conducted after dark. Typically requires Open Water certification as a minimum. You can enforce this with the **Minimum Certification** field.

### Other

Anything that does not fit the above categories — shore dives, freediving, whale-watching combined with diving, etc.

## How Type Is Used

- **Public site** — the type may be shown as a label or category on your booking page (depending on your public site theme).
- **Filtering** — you can filter the Tours list by type.
- **Reporting** — trip and booking reports include the tour type.

## Changing a Tour's Type

You can change the type at any time by editing the tour. The change does not affect existing bookings.
